Electricity
‚ö° Electricity

Electricity is a special kind of energy created when tiny charges (electrons) move through metals. For electricity to flow, we need a complete electric circuit ‚Äî wires, batteries, switches, bulbs. (from the PDF narrative)

Conductors

Allow current to travel through them.

Examples: gold, silver, iron, copper; graphite; salt/acid/alkali water.

Insulators

Do not allow current to pass (bad conductors).

Also: pure/distilled water is a poor conductor.

Action Section
  1. Pour 70 ml pure water into a 120 ml beaker; add 7 g salt.
  2. Stir and complete the circuit with a dry cell/battery.
  3. Check if the bulb glows; add dilute acid/alkali and repeat.

LEDs convert ~95% to light vs ~10% for filament; less heat, more efficiency.
